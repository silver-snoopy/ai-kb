## Design Context

### Users

Solo user (Daniel Sallai) preparing for the **Claude Certified Architect — Foundations (CCA-F)** exam by end of May 2026. Technically skilled Claude Code user with Forward-Deployed-Engineer career goals. Studies across three contexts:

- **Desk, focused session (primary):** 45–90 min blocks at a desktop with Obsidian open, full attention.
- **Phone, quick drill:** 5–15 min between meetings or in transit; wants to chip away without setup.
- **Evening wind-down:** 10pm+ after work, tired, wants something that doesn't feel like more work.

Single user, not shared. No team features, no social features, no leaderboards. The vault is public on GitHub but the study surface is deeply personal.

### Brand Personality

**Three words (user-chosen):** `precise` · `restrained` · `luminous`

**Physical references Daniel named:**
- **IKEA** — Swedish democratic minimalism. Function-first, nothing superfluous, quiet.
- **Herman Miller Embody** — engineered ergonomics with a single purposeful accent (the red pivot). Everything structural, nothing decorative.
- **Audi S3 (interior)** — German understatement. Matte brushed aluminum, matte leather, no chrome for chrome's sake. Instrument lights provide the only warmth.

**Synthesis:** German industrial design meets Nordic minimalism. Every element justifies its existence. The amber accent is load-bearing, not decorative — it only appears on what's actionable.

**Emotional goal:** calm focus. Monk's study. The user should open the app at 10pm and feel settled, not stimulated.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme:** both light + dark with manual toggle (persisted in localStorage). Respect `prefers-color-scheme` as the initial default, but the toggle wins once used.

**Color vocabulary:**
- Near-monochrome surfaces with OKLCH-tuned neutrals tinted slightly warm (toward hue 60).
- One precise accent: `oklch(0.7 0.14 65)` — a warm instrument-amber. Used only on interactive state (hovered/focused/selected) and on the correct-answer reveal. Not used as decoration.
- No secondary accent. No gradients. No color-coded per-domain chips cluttering the chrome (domain labels get small mono badges instead).

**Typography:**
- **Primary family: Chivo** (variable, open-source, by Omnibus Type) — a calm neogrotesque with DIN-adjacent proportions. Not in the overused AI-reflex list.
- **Numeric/badge family: Chivo Mono** (same family, monospace variant) — used for domain IDs (`D1`–`D5`), stat card values, interval labels (`<1d`, `4d`, `2w`), question numbering, source citations.
- One family, two widths. Monotype discipline = restraint.
- Type scale: 5 steps, 1.25 ratio, fixed `rem` (it's an app UI, not marketing).
- Body measure capped at ~65ch for question text.

**Layout:**
- Top nav only. No sidebar (anti-reference: "cluttered admin panel").
- Left-aligned throughout. No centered hero blocks.
- Cards are **flat with a single 1px border**. No box-shadows for decoration. No rounded-corner drop shadows (the AI tell).
- Hairline dividers (1px) between sections instead of spaced gaps + cards.
- Generous vertical rhythm. 4pt spacing scale with semantic tokens.
- Container: `max-w: 720px` reading width, centered horizontally.

**Motion:**
- 150ms `ease-out` on hover/focus. 200ms `ease-out-quart` on card reveal (fade + 4px translate-y).
- No bouncy/elastic easing. No celebration animations. No Duolingo-style flourishes.
- Page transitions: none. Instant state changes. Precision beats smoothness.

**Interaction idioms shared across practice + review:**
- Keyboard-first: Space to reveal / Enter to submit / `1–4` to pick options or rate.
- Active-state is a thin amber underline OR filled amber button. No color bombs.
- Theme toggle: small sun/moon icon, top-right, no label.
- Progress: hairline bar (2px), not thick filled tracks.
- Domain identity: `D1` mono badge with a 4px colored dot. Color used sparingly, not as chip background.

### Design Principles

1. **Load-bearing only.** Every border, color, and motion must do work. If it's decorative, remove it.
2. **Monotype discipline.** One font family, two widths (Chivo + Chivo Mono). No second font just because a brief "needs" one.
3. **The accent is a signal, not a vibe.** Amber appears on interactive state, on correct answers, and nowhere else. That's how it keeps meaning.
4. **Quiet chrome, loud content.** Nav, footer, cards recede. Question text and stats are the typographic event.
5. **Practice and Review are one system, two registers.** Same tokens, same nav, same typography. The only difference is density (quiz is card-per-question; review is card-per-flashcard with reveal affordance) and the rating primitive (4 buttons, not an option list).

### Anti-references (user-confirmed)

- ❌ Duolingo-style gamification (streaks, stars, celebration animations, badges, cartoon illustrations).
- ❌ Cluttered admin-panel aesthetic (sidebars, breadcrumbs everywhere, dense toolbars).
- ❌ Generic SaaS dashboard (purple gradients, cyan accents, rounded generic icons).
- ❌ Copying claudecertifications.com or claudecertificationguide.com. The navigation taxonomy was acceptable; the visual vocabulary is explicitly not.
